Play itch.io ↗

Circuit Bloom is an interactive narrative set in a post-human dystopia where insects have inherited the ruins. They build cities from circuit boards, wire, and plastic scraps humans left behind—adaptation as worldbuilding. The story follows survival, craft, and ecology through a scale we usually ignore.

World & Story

The project mixes visual storytelling with playable interaction: you move through spaces that feel half documentary, half fable. Environments read as tactile and improvised—junk elevated into architecture. Themes of climate aftermath, repurposing, and who gets to thrive after collapse run through every scene.

Production

Built with XR and Unity workflows alongside heavy worldbuilding and art direction. The team treated bugs not as set dressing but as protagonists with their own culture built from our waste. Playable builds and documentation extend the world beyond a single linear cut.

Exhibition

Selected for AWE 2025 (Augmented World Expo)—one of the largest XR showcases globally. Showing there meant pitching an immersive story that could hold up in a professional expo context, not just a classroom prototype.
