Survival of the Smallest was our group final for Core Lab Systems—a fast-paced multiplayer survival game built around multiple player input from the ground up. Set in a chaotic bug-eat-bug ecosystem, you and a friend grab two Xbox controllers, drop into the arena, and fight to win: survive the waves together or compete head-to-head to see who’s the last critter standing.

How It Plays

The game is designed for local multiplayer first. Two controllers map to two players in real time—movement, attacks, and survival pressure all stack as the environment gets harder. You can also play with keyboard (arrow keys, WASD, Shift, Tab, X) or mouse for menus, but controllers are what the experience is tuned for. Every session is about reading your opponent or partner under pressure and trying to come out on top.

My Role

On this project I focused on sound design and the ideology flow of the game—how the world’s tone, tension, and “bug eat bug” logic read through audio and pacing rather than UI alone. I shaped music and sonic feedback so co-op and versus modes each feel distinct: chaos when you’re allied, sharper rivalry when you’re competing for the last spot alive.

Team
